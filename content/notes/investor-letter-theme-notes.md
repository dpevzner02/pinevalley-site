# Investor Letter Theme Notes

## Working Theme

Investor letters as decision-making training.

Possible recurring title:

- Capital Letters
- Owner's Notes
- Letters On Judgment
- The Patient Capital Briefing
- Pine Valley Capital Notes

## Why This Fits Pine Valley

The platform is about using tools, systems, and habits to improve capability. Investor letters add a financial wellbeing and judgment pillar without becoming a stock-tip publication.

The strongest angle is not "what famous investors are buying." The stronger angle is:

How do serious investors think under uncertainty, and what can normal people borrow from that?

## Recurring Issue Structure

1. One core idea from the letters.
2. Three distilled lessons.
3. One practical application outside investing.
4. One experiment for readers.
5. Source list.

## Guardrails

- Do not present stock recommendations.
- Avoid copying long passages from letters.
- Use short quotes only when necessary.
- Focus on durable mental models, not market predictions.
- Separate summary from interpretation.

## Good First Topics

- Owner mindset vs price watching
- Patience as an active skill
- Risk as permanent impairment, not volatility
- Incentives and trust
- Information diet and objectivity
- Admitting mistakes early
- Why quality matters, but price still matters
